How to tell if a viral video is fake | Video | Kids News Hide Search Search CBC Kids News SEARCH CBC Kids News Hot Topics Topics KN Explains #GoodNews #WeirdNews Animals Gaming Sports Indigenous Stories Climate Change Mental Health War in Ukraine Science Search How to tell if a viral video is fake Story by Ari Resnick and CBC Kids News • 2018-11-22 07:00 What deepfake videos are and why they can be dangerous Did he really just say that? Probably not, if what we know about deepfake videos is true. Deepfake videos are videos that make it look like somebody is saying or doing something, when they never actually did. They’re usually made with a kind of artificial intelligence technology that makes it possible to turn a still photograph into a moving avatar. Kind of cool, right? Except when those videos circulate online and people believe what they’re seeing is true. An investigation by CBC’s The Fifth Estate showed deepfakes can ruin people’s lives and even influence who wins an election. Host Mark Kelley spoke with CBC Kids News contributor Ari Resnick about what he learned. Play the video at the top of the page to hear more.
