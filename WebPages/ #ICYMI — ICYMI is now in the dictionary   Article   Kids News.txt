#ICYMI — ICYMI is now in the dictionary | Article | Kids News Hide Search Search CBC Kids News SEARCH CBC Kids News Hot Topics Topics KN Explains #GoodNews #WeirdNews Animals Gaming Sports Indigenous Stories Climate Change Mental Health War in Ukraine Science Search #ICYMI — ICYMI is now in the dictionary Story by CBC Kids News • 2022-09-16 14:16 Other new additions include yeet, sus and pumpkin spice In case you missed it, ICYMI was added to the Merriam-Webster dictionary on Sept. 7, along with 369 other words. ICYMI means in case you missed it. It’s an acronym, which is a shortened version of a name or phrase made up of the first letters of words. Acronyms are used a lot in the English language. Even CBC is an acronym. It’s short for Canadian Broadcasting Corporation. Merriam-Webster is a dictionary that has been published since 1828. Dictionaries are updated regularly and new words are chosen based on popular culture. According to Merriam-Webster, a word can be added to their dictionary “when it is used by many people who all agree that it means the same thing.” This is usually done once a year. Here are some other additions you may be familiar with already: Yeet: To throw an object with great force. Pumpkin spice: A popular coffee flavour. Sus: Suspicious. Cringe: Something embarrassing or awkward. Greenwash: To make a product appear more environmentally friendly than it actually is.
